  the UN for the First Time as a National Human Rights Institution (NHRI)
date: 2026-02-15T09:14:00
perex: <p>On Wednesday, 11 February 2026, a dialogue between the Czech Republic
  and the UN Committee on the Elimination of Discrimination against Women took
  place in Geneva. The Committee monitors compliance with the Convention on the
  Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination against Women (CEDAW), which the
  Czech Republic has ratified and thereby committed itself to protecting women
  and girls from discrimination in many areas of life. The Office of the Public
  Defender of Rights and the Children’s Ombudsman participated in this process
  as the National Human Rights Institution (NHRI).</p>

<p>In order to provide the UN Committee with a comprehensive picture of the state of gender equality in the Czech Republic, the Ombudsman, in his capacity as NHRI, submitted an independent report to the Committee. The Committee drew directly on this report in several instances when addressing questions to the Czech delegation. The Ombudsman primarily highlighted issues that received less attention during the discussion or complemented the contributions of non-governmental organisations. His input was based on his own findings.</p>
<p>
<strong>The Ombudsman’s independent report addressed the following areas:</strong>
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Insufficient assessment of the impact of legislation on gender equality
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The number and success rate of anti-discrimination lawsuits filed by women
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Insufficient funding of NGOs assisting victims of crime
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The situation of migrant women and the detention of families with children
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The impact of motherhood and parenthood on women’s employment
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The consequences of insufficient support for women caregivers
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chronic fatigue syndrome – unequal access of women to healthcare and social support
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mental health of girls and young women and limited access to healthcare
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Healthcare related to pregnancy and childbirth
<br>•&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Compensation for unlawful sterilisation</p>
<p>In the report, the Ombudsman also proposed specific solutions.</p>
